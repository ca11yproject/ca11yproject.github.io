CA11y Project

CA11y (Content Accessibility: Highly Individualised Digital Content for Supporting Diverse Needs) is funded through an EPSRC New Investigator Award (EP/X012395/1). CA11y seeks to improve access to digital content through innovative human-centred technologies.

The project is led by Principal Investigator Dr Timothy Neate (Human-Centred Computing, Department of Informatics, King’s College London) and Co-Investigator Prof Madeline Cruice (Department of Language and Communication Science, City, University of London) and will run from October 2023 to October 2025. It is supported by a wider team of academic and non-academic partners.
